Key Findings

1 Cash Out adoption is 2–5× higher than Early Win across all six markets (Brazil VIP: Cash Out 46% vs Early Win 9%; Ecuador Non-VIP: 23% vs 8.9%)
2 Brazil has the largest untapped pool — 39.4% of VIPs cash out but don't use Early Win (VIP Cash Out at 46%, only ~14% of Cash Out users have ever tried Early Win)
3 VIPs adopt Early Win at ~1.5–2.6× the Non-VIP rate across markets (Mexico: 19.4% VIP vs 10.7% Non-VIP; Chile: 14.9% vs 7.8%)
4 Mexico leads with 19.4% VIP and 10.7% Non-VIP Early Win adoption — the highest anywhere (28% of Non-VIP Cash Out users also try Early Win, ~1.8× the cross-market average)
5 Ireland has the lowest Cash Out adoption of any market, with Non-VIP Early Win at just 3.7% (Non-VIP Cash Out 10.2% — the lowest; VIP Cash Out 27.8% — below all other markets' VIP levels)

Greece

Greece — Key Takeaways

Cash Out dominates; Early Win remains niche.
Cash Out adoption is 3–4× higher than Early Win across both segments.

Early Win users already know Cash Out — not the reverse.
Most Early Win adopters also use Cash Out (~43% Non-VIP, ~67% VIP), but the vast majority of Cash Out users have never tried Early Win.

VIPs engage more with both features.
VIP adoption is roughly double Non-VIP for both features, with higher overlap — VIPs are more willing to experiment.

Biggest growth opportunity: convert Cash Out users to Early Win.
Over 80% of Cash Out users haven't tried Early Win — and they already understand acting on a live bet.

Brazil

Brazil — Key Takeaways

Widest Cash Out/Early Win gap of any market — Cash Out is 5× larger.
Early Win adoption sits at just ~3.4% Non-VIP while Cash Out reaches ~18%; among VIPs, Cash Out hits ~46% vs Early Win at ~9%.

Early Win users know Cash Out, but not the reverse.
Over half of Early Win adopters also use Cash Out (~53% Non-VIP, ~73% VIP), yet only ~10% of Cash Out users have tried Early Win.

VIPs amplify both features equally — the 5× gap persists.
VIP adoption is ~2.5× Non-VIP for both Early Win and Cash Out, but the ratio between them stays flat, unlike markets where VIPs narrow the gap.

Largest untapped cross-sell pool: ~16% are Cash Out-only users.
Brazil's massive Cash Out base combined with the lowest Early Win penetration makes it the highest-potential market for Early Win conversion campaigns.

Mexico

Mexico — Key Takeaways

Narrowest Cash Out/Early Win gap — Mexico leads Early Win adoption.
Cash Out is only ~2× Early Win here (vs 3–5× elsewhere). Non-VIP Early Win at ~10.7% and VIP Early Win at ~19.4% are the highest of any market.

Strongest feature overlap — users engage with both.
~28% of Non-VIP Cash Out users also try Early Win (vs ~16% in Greece, ~10% in Brazil). VIP overlap reaches ~13.5%, the highest anywhere.

VIPs reach ~19% Early Win adoption — proof the feature can scale.
Mexico VIPs show that with the right product-market fit, nearly 1 in 5 users will adopt Early Win alongside Cash Out.

Mexico is the model market — study what drives higher Early Win uptake.
Its unique position (highest Early Win, highest overlap, narrowest gap) suggests product or market factors that other countries should replicate.

Chile

Chile — Key Takeaways

Moderate Cash Out/Early Win ratio (~2.7×) with balanced user split.
Non-VIP Early Win sits at ~7.8% vs Cash Out at ~21%. Early Win-only (~3.8%) and overlap (~4%) are nearly equal — a rare balanced split.

Early Win attracts a distinct user type, not just Cash Out spillover.
The near 50/50 split between Early Win-only and overlap users in Non-VIP suggests Early Win appeals to a segment that doesn't naturally gravitate to Cash Out.

VIP overlap surges — most VIP Early Win users also Cash Out.
VIP overlap reaches ~10.9% (of ~14.9% Early Win adoption), meaning ~73% of VIP Early Win users are dual-feature adopters.

Non-VIP "Early Win-only" cohort is a unique conversion opportunity.
Unlike other markets, Chile's Early Win-only users (~3.8%) are sizable — introducing them to Cash Out could lift overall engagement.

Ecuador

Ecuador — Key Takeaways

Cash Out/Early Win ratio (~2.6×) mirrors Greece, but at higher absolute rates.
Non-VIP Early Win is ~8.9% and Cash Out is ~23%; both exceed Greece's levels while maintaining a similar relative gap.

VIP overlap doesn't scale like other markets.
Early Win-to-Cash Out crossover is ~54% in Non-VIP and ~55% in VIP — virtually flat, unlike Greece or Brazil where VIPs show a much higher crossover jump.

Highest "Early Win-Only" VIP share (~5.8%) — some VIPs prefer Early Win exclusively.
Ecuador VIPs who use Early Win without Cash Out are notably more common than in other markets, suggesting a distinct user preference.

Understand what drives exclusive Early Win loyalty among VIPs.
Ecuador's unique Early Win-only cohort could reveal product features or betting behaviours that make Early Win independently valuable — insights for other markets.

Ireland

Ireland — Key Takeaways

Lowest adoption for both features — a market penetration challenge.
Non-VIP Early Win (~3.7%) and Cash Out (~10.2%) are the lowest of any country; even VIP Cash Out (~27.8%) trails other markets' Non-VIP rates.

The Cash Out/Early Win ratio (~2.8×) is normal — adoption is low for both, not skewed.
Unlike Brazil (5× gap), Ireland's challenge isn't Early Win specifically — it's overall feature engagement across the board.

VIP crossover behaves like mature markets.
~71% of VIP Early Win users also Cash Out, on par with Chile and Brazil — VIPs who try Early Win are equally engaged here.

Priority: drive broader feature awareness before optimising cross-sell.
With both features under-penetrated, Ireland needs visibility and education campaigns rather than Early Win-to-Cash Out conversion tactics.

All Countries

All Countries — Key Takeaways

Cash Out consistently 2–5× higher than Early Win — the gap is universal.
Average Cash Out adoption is ~17% Non-VIP and ~41% VIP; Early Win sits at ~5.3% and ~10.8% respectively.

Mexico is the clear standout — narrowest gap, highest Early Win, highest overlap.
With Early Win at ~10.7% Non-VIP and ~19.4% VIP, Mexico proves the feature can reach meaningful scale; its success factors deserve investigation.

VIP status doubles adoption but widens the Cash Out/Early Win ratio.
The gap grows from ~3.2× Non-VIP to ~3.8× VIP on average — VIPs disproportionately favour Cash Out, making Early Win's VIP growth an untapped opportunity.

Converting just 10% of Cash Out-only users would roughly double Early Win adoption.
Cash Out-only users average ~14.5% Non-VIP and ~33% VIP — they already understand acting on a live bet, making them the ideal Early Win target.

Key Findings

1 Cash Out per-bet rate is 3–6× higher than Early Win — the user-level gap carries through to bet level (Greece: 7.0% vs 2.2% Non-VIP; Brazil: 9.2% vs 1.7% Non-VIP)
2 VIP status lifts Cash Out bet rates but Early Win stays flat at ~1.7% in both markets (VIP Cash Out: 7.4% Greece, 10.1% Brazil; VIP Early Win: 1.7% in both)
3 Early Win per-bet rate is remarkably stable at ~1.3–2.4% across markets, segments, and core bet types (excl. low-volume System bets)
4 Brazil VIP Early Win bet rate grew +65% in 8 weeks despite flat user adoption (VIP BetBuilderCombo Cash Out averages ~11%, while Early Win on the same type sits at just 1.5%)

Greece

Greece — Key Takeaways

Cash Out bet rate is 3–4× higher than Early Win across all bet types.
Overall Cash Out is ~7.0% Non-VIP and ~7.4% VIP vs Early Win at ~2.2% and ~1.7% respectively.

VIPs Cash Out more per bet but use Early Win less.
VIP Early Win bet rate (~1.7%) is actually lower than Non-VIP (~2.2%) — VIP status boosts Cash Out usage at the bet level, not Early Win.

System bets have the widest Cash Out/Early Win gap (~5.4× Non-VIP, ~8.5× VIP).
System bets reach ~8.6% Cash Out Non-VIP and ~9.4% VIP, suggesting complex bet types favour Cash Out disproportionately.

Early Win engagement is bet-type agnostic — it doesn't spike for any category.
Early Win rates hover at 1.6–2.4% regardless of bet type, while Cash Out varies more — implying Early Win usage is driven by user behaviour, not bet construction.

Brazil

Brazil — Key Takeaways

Steepest Cash Out/Early Win gap at bet level — 5–6× across all views.
Non-VIP Cash Out is ~9.2% vs Early Win at ~1.7% (~5.4×); VIP Cash Out rises to ~10.1% while Early Win stays flat at ~1.7% (~5.9×).

Early Win bet rate is identical across VIP and Non-VIP (~1.7%).
Unlike user-level adoption (which doubles for VIPs), bet-level Early Win usage is flat — VIPs who adopt Early Win don't apply it to more bets.

BetBuilderCombo drives the highest Cash Out rates (~10–11%).
Complex combo bets see the steepest Cash Out engagement in both segments, while Early Win is lowest on this type (~1.3% Non-VIP).

Brazil's Cash Out bet rates exceed Greece by ~30% — higher per-bet engagement.
At 9–10% vs Greece's 7–7.4%, Brazil's bettors Cash Out more frequently per bet, not just per user, deepening the Early Win gap.

All Countries (Only Greece and Brazil)

All Countries — Key Takeaways

Cash Out dominates at bet level in both markets — but the gap varies.
Greece's Cash Out/Early Win ratio is ~3.2× while Brazil's is ~5.4×; Brazil bettors Cash Out more frequently per bet (9.2% vs 7.0%).

Early Win bet rates are remarkably similar (~1.3–2.4%) despite very different user adoption.
User-level adoption diverges (Greece 6% vs Brazil 3.4%), but per-bet Early Win usage converges — adoptive users apply Early Win at similar rates.

VIP status lifts Cash Out bet rates but leaves Early Win flat.
VIP Cash Out rises to ~7.4% (Greece) and ~10.1% (Brazil) vs Non-VIP, while Early Win stays at ~1.7% in both — the per-bet VIP effect is Cash Out-only.

Complex bets amplify Cash Out but not Early Win — bet type matters for Cash Out only.
System and BetBuilderCombo show the highest Cash Out rates; Early Win is flat across types, suggesting Early Win triggers are independent of bet complexity.

Key Findings

1 Early Win bets average 8.4–9.7 legs (35–55% more than market average); Cash Out bets carry the highest stakes (Greece EW: 8.4 legs vs 6.0 avg; Brazil EW: 9.7 legs vs 7.2 avg)
2 Cash Out is the revenue anchor — Greek VIP Cash Out averages €52.61 per bet (Non-VIP Cash Out €14.3 in Greece, €7.3 in Brazil; Early Win trails at €7.5 and €4.3 respectively)
3 Two bettor profiles: Early Win users build long, lower-stake slips; Cash Out users bet short slips with higher stakes (Greece Non-VIP: EW €7.5 at 8.4 legs vs CO €14.3 at 5.5 legs)
4 VIPs stake ~4× more than Non-VIPs but leg counts stay the same across both segments (Greece VIP: CO €52.6, EW €38, All €32 — yet leg counts nearly identical to Non-VIP)
5 BB Combo Early Win produces the longest slips in both markets at ~11–13 legs (Brazil BB Combo EW reaches ~13 legs; Greece ~11 legs)
6 Multiple bets amplify the Cash Out stake premium most — €15.7 in Greece (~1.8× average), €7.3 in Brazil (~1.7×) (VIP Multiple CO in Greece: €57+, the single highest stake figure observed)

Greece

Greece — Key Takeaways

Early Win is the long-accumulator feature — ~8.4 legs vs ~6 average.
Early Win bets have ~40% more legs than all bets in both segments, while Cash Out bets are closer to average (~5.5 Non-VIP, ~5.1 VIP).

Cash Out captures the big-stake bets — Early Win sits below average.
Non-VIP Cash Out stakes are ~€14.3 (~1.7× average), while Early Win stakes (~€7.5) are actually below the €8.3 market average.

VIPs bet 4× larger but the structural pattern holds.
VIP stakes scale up proportionally (Cash Out ~€52.6, Early Win ~€38, all ~€32) while leg counts remain virtually identical to Non-VIP.

Early Win appeals to complexity-seekers; Cash Out appeals to value-seekers.
Early Win users build longer, lower-stake slips; Cash Out users bet shorter slips with higher stakes — two distinct bettor profiles.

Brazil

Brazil — Key Takeaways

Brazil builds longer accumulators than Greece across the board.
Early Win bets average ~9.7 legs (vs Greece ~8.4), all bets ~7.2 (vs ~6.0) — Brazil's market naturally favours more selections per slip.

Early Win stakes match the market average — no premium, unlike Cash Out.
Non-VIP Early Win stakes (~€4.3) sit at parity with the €4.3 average, while Cash Out stakes (~€7.3) are ~1.7× higher — Cash Out is the "big bet" feature.

VIPs play shorter slips but stake ~4× more.
VIP Early Win drops to ~8.9 legs (from ~9.7 Non-VIP) while stakes jump to ~€25.6 — VIPs trade complexity for bigger wagers.

Lower nominal stakes than Greece — but the same structural pattern.
Brazil's overall stakes are ~50% of Greece's, yet the Cash Out > Early Win > All hierarchy and VIP multiplier (~4×) are identical across both markets.

All Countries (Only Greece and Brazil)

All Countries — Key Takeaways

Early Win = long slips, Cash Out = big stakes — the pattern is universal.
In both markets, Early Win bets have the most legs (~8.4–9.7) while Cash Out bets carry the highest stakes (~€7.3–14.3 Non-VIP).

Brazil builds longer accumulators; Greece bets bigger per slip.
Brazil adds ~1.2 more legs across all categories, but Greece stakes are ~2× higher — a clear market-level difference in betting style.

VIPs shorten slips slightly but stake ~4× more — in both markets.
VIP leg counts dip below Non-VIP, but the ~4× VIP stake multiplier is remarkably consistent across Greece and Brazil.

Two distinct user profiles emerge: Early Win = complexity-seeker, Cash Out = value-seeker.
These profiles hold across both markets and segments — Early Win and Cash Out serve fundamentally different bettor needs.
